How do we grade?


We grade a variety of our food products based on Australias food safety standard (MLA.

Food safety standards place obligations on Australian food businesses to produce food that is safe and suitable to eat.

Our business is any business or activity that involves the handling of any type of food for sale, or the sale of food in Australia.

We place the up most priority to bring you the most affordable cuts direct from farmer to Businesses.



Beef
How we grade beef?

We grade beef using a fixed measuring chart which determines the quality of the (BMS) Beef Marbling Score and the yield rate of the beef.

The yield rate indicates how much meat can be taken out from the carcass of the cow.

The (BMS) Beef Marbling Score indicates the amout of intermuscular fat a piece of meat contains, taking into consideration the cut of the beef.













Pork
How we grade pork?

Grading pork is a little different compared to grading beef. Pork is graded based on several qualities the meat possess.

The first condition of grading would be the "Last Rib Backfat Thickness"(LRBF) which is the thickness of the fat located at the spinne of the pig.

The second condition of grading would be the degree of muscling of the meat, comparing to beef instead of fat marbling it is more desirable if the pork contain less marble due to the meat containing a higher concentration of fat.

The third condition of grading would be the quality of fats the meat contains. It is the combination of both the (LRBF) and the degree of muscling the meat contains, the fats of the higher quality animal tend to be able to melt and render at a lower temperature.



Poultry
How we grade poultry?

Grading poultry has two main conditions which are the size of the bird and the condition of the bird after processing.

Grading the size is highly dependent on multiple factors which are the species of bird, the age of the bird and the living condition of the bird. Older and more mature birds tend to be less tender compared to yourger aged birds of the same species. Size is also sometimes noticable between different species of birds taking their age into consideration.

Poultry is graded into 4 categories which is grade A, grade B, grade C and No grade. Grade A would be considered to be in pristine condition without any defects. Grade B would contain minor defects but still in good condition. Grade C would be those chicken with damages meat and with average meat quality. Finally, No grade would be the worse where there are limbs missing and trim of the chicken are left.
